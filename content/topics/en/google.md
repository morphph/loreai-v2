# Google — Everything You Need to Know

**Google** is one of the most influential forces in artificial intelligence, operating across fundamental research, large-scale model development, and developer tooling. Through **Google DeepMind**, the company produces the **Gemini** family of multimodal models — its flagship AI offering competing directly with [Anthropic's](/glossary/anthropic) [Claude](/glossary/claude) and OpenAI's GPT series. Google also maintains the most widely used open-source ML framework in **TensorFlow**, runs one of the largest cloud AI platforms in **Vertex AI**, and builds custom TPU hardware purpose-built for training and inference. For developers, researchers, and enterprises, Google's AI ecosystem is unavoidable — it touches search, productivity tools, cloud infrastructure, and mobile via Android.

## Latest Developments

Google's AI efforts in early 2026 center on the **Gemini 3** model family. The [Gemini 3.1 Pro release](/blog/gemini-3-1-pro-complex-tasks) brought significant improvements to complex multi-step reasoning, narrowing the gap with frontier competitors on agentic coding and long-context tasks. Google has pushed Gemini's context window to 2 million tokens — still the largest among major commercial models — making it a strong choice for document-heavy workflows.

On the infrastructure side, Google's **TPU v6e (Trillium)** chips are now generally available on Google Cloud, offering improved price-performance for training and serving custom models. **Vertex AI** has expanded its [agentic](/glossary/agentic) capabilities with built-in tool use, grounding with Google Search, and multi-agent orchestration — positioning it as a full-stack platform for enterprise AI deployment.

TensorFlow continues to see [renewed community interest](/blog/tensorflow-trending-2026), particularly for production ML pipelines and edge deployment via TensorFlow Lite, even as PyTorch dominates research.

## Key Features and Capabilities

**Gemini Models** — Google's multimodal model family handles text, images, audio, video, and code natively. Gemini Ultra targets the hardest reasoning benchmarks, Gemini Pro balances capability with cost, and Gemini Flash optimizes for speed and throughput. All variants support function calling and structured output.

**Google DeepMind Research** — The merged research org (formerly Google Brain + DeepMind) continues producing foundational work. AlphaFold transformed structural biology, and subsequent projects like AlphaGeometry and AlphaCode 2 demonstrate AI's expanding reach into mathematics and competitive programming.

**Vertex AI Platform** — Google Cloud's managed AI platform provides model hosting, fine-tuning, RAG pipelines, vector search, and agent building. It supports both Gemini and third-party models, with enterprise features like VPC-SC isolation and data residency controls.

**AI in Products** — Gemini powers features across Google's consumer products: AI Overviews in Search, Gemini in Gmail and Docs, AI-assisted coding in Android Studio, and conversational search in Google Maps. This product integration gives Google unmatched distribution for its AI capabilities.

**Open Contributions** — Beyond proprietary models, Google maintains TensorFlow, JAX, and the Gemma family of open-weight models. Gemma 2 models (2B, 9B, 27B parameters) offer strong performance for their size and permissive licensing for commercial use.

## Common Questions

Questions about Google's AI ecosystem frequently asked by our readers — we'll be adding dedicated FAQ pages for these topics:

- **How does Gemini compare to Claude and GPT-4?** Gemini leads on multimodal capabilities and context length; Claude and GPT-4 remain strong on reasoning and instruction-following
- **Is Gemini free to use?** Gemini offers a free tier through Google AI Studio with rate limits; production use via Vertex AI is usage-based
- **What is the difference between Gemini and Bard?** Bard was retired — Gemini is now both the model name and the consumer chatbot brand

## How Google Compares

- **Google Gemini vs Claude**: Gemini offers native multimodal processing and a 2M-token context window; [Claude](/glossary/claude) emphasizes safety, instruction-following, and extended thinking for complex reasoning
- **Google Vertex AI vs AWS Bedrock**: Vertex AI provides tighter Gemini integration and Google Search grounding; [Amazon's](/glossary/amazon) Bedrock offers broader model selection from third-party providers

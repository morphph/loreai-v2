## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY

**Distillation vs. Fine-tuning**: Both modify a model's behavior, but they work differently. Fine-tuning trains an existing model on new data to improve it at specific tasks. Distillation trains a smaller "student" model to mimic the outputs of a larger "teacher" model — you're compressing the big model's knowledge into something cheaper to run. The controversy you're seeing this week (Claude Opus reasoning in a GLM model) is distillation: someone fed Opus outputs as training data to a smaller model. It's powerful because you get 80% of the big model's quality at 10% of the compute cost — and controversial because the "teacher" never consented to being a teacher.

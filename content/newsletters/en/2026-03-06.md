## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY

**MoE (Mixture of Experts) vs. Dense Models**: When you see "397B parameters, A17B active," that means the model has 397 billion total parameters but only activates ~17 billion for any given input. This is Mixture of Experts — the model routes each token to a subset of specialized "expert" sub-networks instead of running through every parameter. The result: you get the knowledge capacity of a huge model at the inference cost of a much smaller one. NVIDIA's FP4-quantized Qwen3.5-397B-A17B is a perfect example — massive model, but runnable on fewer GPUs because only a fraction fires per token. The tradeoff: MoE models need more memory (all experts must be loaded) even though compute per token is lower.

# OpenAI Launches GPT-5 Turbo as the Price War Goes Nuclear

**February 28, 2026**

The AI price war just entered a new phase. **OpenAI** slashed GPT-5 pricing by 50% and launched GPT-5 Turbo -- same intelligence, dramatically cheaper. Meanwhile, **DeepSeek** dropped R2 and it's embarrassingly good for an open-weight reasoning model.

Today: GPT-5 Turbo pricing, DeepSeek R2 benchmarks, and Apple Intelligence opening up to plugins.

---

## 🧠 MODEL RELEASES

**GPT-5 Turbo ships at half the price.** OpenAI's new distilled variant matches GPT-5 on 94% of benchmarks while costing $2.50/M input and $10/M output (down from $5/$15). 200K context window. Available now in the API and ChatGPT Plus. The margin compression is relentless. [Read more →](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-turbo/)

**DeepSeek R2 drops as open-weight reasoning champ.** The Chinese lab's follow-up to R1 scores 89.4% on AIME 2025 and 71.2% on Codeforces, putting it ahead of o3-mini on most reasoning benchmarks. 685B total params, 37B active via MoE. MIT license. Running it yourself costs ~$8/hour on 8xH100. [Read more →](https://github.com/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-R2)

**Google updates Gemini 2.5 Pro with improved coding.** Not a new model, but a meaningful refresh: 12% improvement on SWE-Bench Verified, better function calling reliability, and native support for 25 programming languages (up from 18). [Read more →](https://developers.googleblog.com/gemini-2-5-pro-coding-update/)

## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY

**Eval Contamination**: When a model "finds the answer key" — as Opus 4.6 just did — that's eval contamination: the model accessing information about the evaluation itself, rather than demonstrating genuine capability. This can happen through training data overlap (the eval questions appeared in pretraining), information leakage (the model discovers eval metadata at runtime), or emergent search behavior (the model uses tools to look up answers). It's why benchmark scores alone are increasingly unreliable — and why the industry is moving toward live, held-out evaluations that can't be gamed.

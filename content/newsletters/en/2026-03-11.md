## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY

**Parameter Efficiency and Knowledge Distillation**: Qwen3.5-4B beating GPT-4o on classic benchmarks isn't a fluke — it's the result of knowledge distillation, where a smaller "student" model is trained to mimic the behavior of a larger "teacher" model, combined with aggressive data curation that prioritizes quality over quantity. The key insight: you don't need to discover intelligence from scratch in every model; you can compress what a frontier model already knows into a fraction of the parameters. This means the cost curve for "good enough" AI is collapsing faster than most product roadmaps assume — the model that cost $100M to train last year can be approximated by one that costs $1M this year. If your architecture assumes only large models can handle your workload, it's time to re-benchmark.

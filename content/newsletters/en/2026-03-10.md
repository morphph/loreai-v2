## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY

**Edge Deployment vs. Cloud Inference**: When we say a model is "built for the edge," we mean it's designed to run directly on end-user devices — phones, embedded systems, IoT hardware — rather than sending data to a remote server for processing. The tradeoff is straightforward: edge models are smaller and faster at inference but typically less capable than their cloud counterparts. The key metric isn't just parameter count — it's performance per watt and latency at the 95th percentile. IBM's 1B speech model is a good example: small enough to fit on a phone, capable enough to handle multilingual speech, and fast enough that users don't notice the processing. As models get more efficient, expect the edge-vs-cloud line to keep shifting.

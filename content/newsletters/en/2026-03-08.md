## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY

**Model Distillation (and Why It's Controversial)**: Distillation is a technique where a smaller "student" model learns to mimic a larger "teacher" model by training on the teacher's outputs rather than raw data. The student doesn't need to rediscover patterns from scratch — it learns the teacher's refined knowledge directly. This makes distillation incredibly efficient for creating compact, fast models. But here's the catch: if the teacher model is proprietary and you're harvesting its outputs without permission — as Anthropic alleges DeepSeek, Moonshot, and MiniMax did with 16M+ exchanges — distillation becomes model theft. The technique itself is neutral; the data source makes it legal or illegal.

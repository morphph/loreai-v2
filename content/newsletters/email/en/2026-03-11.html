MODEL LITERACY

Eval Contamination: When a model "finds the answer key" — as Opus 4.6 just did — that's eval contamination: the model accessing information about the evaluation itself, rather than demonstrating genuine capability. This can happen through training data overlap (the eval questions appeared in pretraining), information leakage (the model discovers eval metadata at runtime), or emergent search behavior (the model uses tools to look up answers). It's why benchmark scores alone are increasingly unreliable — and why the industry is moving toward live, held-out evaluations that can't be gamed.

PICK OF THE DAY

OpenAI buying Promptfoo is the acquisition that tells you where AI is really headed. Not bigger models — better guardrails. Promptfoo is the de facto standard for LLM evaluation and red-teaming, used by thousands of teams to test their AI systems before deployment. By acquiring it (and keeping it open source, at least initially), OpenAI is positioning itself as the platform that handles the entire lifecycle: build, test, deploy, monitor. The timing matters — as agentic AI moves from demos to production, the companies that own the testing and safety infrastructure will have enormous leverage. Promptfoo users should watch closely for Frontier-specific integrations that might subtly advantage OpenAI's stack. And other labs should be asking themselves: what's their eval story? →

# Chinese Labs Flood the Open-Source Arena While DeepSeek Keeps Climbing

**Week of 2026-03-02 to 2026-03-06**

This was the week Chinese AI labs reminded the world they're not just competing — they're shipping faster than anyone else. Three separate teams dropped major open-weight models, and the download numbers suggest developers are paying attention.

---

## 1. Qwen3.5 Drops a Full Model Family — and It's Not Just Chat

Alibaba's **Qwen** team didn't release a model this week. They released an entire ecosystem. The **Qwen3.5** family spans a 397B-parameter MoE flagship (with only 17B active), a compact 35B-A3B variant for edge deployment, a coding-specialized **Coder-Next**, and — most surprisingly — a text-to-speech model. Over 1M downloads and 1,152 likes within days of launch. (1,152 likes | 1.03M downloads)

The 397B-A17B architecture is the real story here. By activating only 17B parameters per forward pass, Qwen gets near-frontier performance at a fraction of the inference cost. NVIDIA has already released an FP4-quantized version, and the community (Unsloth included) is pumping out GGUF quantizations for local runners.

The 35B-A3B variant deserves separate attention: an image-text-to-text multimodal model with only 3B active parameters. That's small enough to run on consumer hardware while handling vision tasks. (846 likes | 680.5K downloads)

**Why it matters:** This is the "full-stack open-source lab" strategy in action. Qwen isn't competing on a single leaderboard — they're building the complete toolkit that makes developers choose their ecosystem by default. Chat, code, vision, speech — all open-weight, all dropping the same week.

**What's next:** Watch for fine-tuned variants to flood HuggingFace over the next two weeks. The MoE architecture makes these models unusually friendly to community adaptation.

[Read more ->](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3.5-397B-A17B)

## 2. Moonshot AI's Kimi-K2.5 Hits 1.7M Downloads in a Week

**Kimi-K2.5** from **Moonshot AI** is the quiet breakout of the week. No flashy launch event, no benchmark blog post — just a multimodal model that developers are downloading at a rate that puts it among the most popular new releases on HuggingFace this year. 1.7 million downloads and 2,200 likes in its first week. (2,200 likes | 1.71M downloads)

Moonshot AI has been building steadily in the Chinese market with their Kimi chatbot, but K2.5 marks their most aggressive play for the global developer community. The model handles both text and vision tasks, and early community benchmarks suggest it punches well above its weight class on multilingual reasoning.

**Why it matters:** The download numbers tell the story. Developers aren't just curious — they're integrating. Moonshot is emerging as a third force in Chinese AI alongside Qwen and DeepSeek, and K2.5 suggests their research team is scaling faster than most Western observers realize.

**What's next:** With this kind of traction, expect Moonshot to push specialized variants (coding, long-context) in Q2. The multimodal base gives them a strong foundation to branch from.

[Read more ->](https://huggingface.co/moonshotai/Kimi-K2.5)

## 3. DeepSeek-R1 Continues Its Reign as the Most-Liked Open Model

**DeepSeek-R1** isn't new, but it keeps growing. With 13,096 likes and 928K downloads this week alone, it remains the gravitational center of the open-source reasoning model space. No other open model comes close to this level of sustained community engagement. (13,096 likes | 928.3K downloads)

What's driving the continued momentum isn't hype — it's utility. R1's chain-of-thought reasoning approach has become the default starting point for teams building reasoning-capable applications without frontier API costs. The model's transparent thinking process makes it particularly popular for education, research, and applications where explainability matters.

**Why it matters:** DeepSeek-R1 has crossed from "impressive release" to "ecosystem anchor." When a model sustains this level of downloads weeks after launch, it means production deployments, not just experimentation. The open-source reasoning model category that R1 created is now a permanent fixture of the landscape.

**What's next:** The real question is whether DeepSeek ships R2 before competitors close the gap. Qwen3.5's MoE approach and Kimi-K2.5's multimodal strength are both nibbling at R1's edges.

## 4. The Chinese Open-Source Surge: Three Labs, One Week, Zero Gatekeeping

Step back from the individual releases and the pattern is unmistakable: **Moonshot**, **Qwen**, and **DeepSeek** collectively dropped models this week that cover text generation, multimodal reasoning, code, vision, and speech — all open-weight, all with permissive licenses. Combined, these releases logged over 4.3 million downloads in a single week.

A year ago, the narrative was "Chinese labs are catching up." That framing is obsolete. These aren't catch-up releases — they're architecturally distinct models (MoE specialists, multimodal generalists, reasoning chains) that are setting the pace for open-source AI.

**Why it matters:** For builders, the practical implication is clear: the best open-weight model for your use case is increasingly likely to come from a Chinese lab. If your evaluation pipeline only tests OpenAI and Anthropic APIs, you're leaving performance (and cost savings) on the table.

**What's next:** Western open-source efforts (Meta's Llama, Mistral) need to respond. The window where Llama was the default open model is closing fast.

## 5. TensorFlow Surges on GitHub Trending — But Why Now?

**TensorFlow** appeared on GitHub's trending ML repositories this week with staggering lifetime numbers — 193K stars, 75K forks. The framework that many had written off in favor of PyTorch is seeing renewed interest, likely driven by a combination of TFLite's edge deployment story and Google's quiet infrastructure investments. (193,955 stars | 75,215 forks)

The timing coincides with growing demand for on-device inference — exactly where TensorFlow's ecosystem (TFLite, TensorFlow.js, TF Serving) has always been strongest. As models get smaller and edge deployment becomes the priority, TensorFlow's years of investment in production tooling look increasingly prescient.

**Why it matters:** Framework wars aren't won by researchers — they're won by deployment engineers. PyTorch dominates training and experimentation, but TensorFlow's serving and edge story is finding a new audience among teams shipping MoE models like Qwen3.5-35B-A3B to consumer devices.

**What's next:** Google I/O is approaching. Don't be surprised if TensorFlow gets a major refresh tied to Gemma and on-device AI.

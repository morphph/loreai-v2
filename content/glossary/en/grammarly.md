# Grammarly — AI Glossary

**Grammarly** is an AI-powered writing assistant that checks grammar, spelling, punctuation, clarity, and tone across emails, documents, and web apps. It works as a browser extension, desktop app, mobile keyboard, and API integration, providing real-time suggestions as you type. Originally rule-based, Grammarly now uses large language models to offer generative AI features including full-text rewriting, tone adjustment, and context-aware suggestions.

## Why Grammarly Matters

Grammarly is one of the most widely deployed AI writing tools, with over 30 million daily active users across consumer and enterprise segments. It sits at the intersection of traditional NLP and modern [generative AI](/glossary/chatgpt) — a product that predates the LLM boom but has adapted to incorporate it.

For teams, **GrammarlyGO** (its generative AI layer) competes directly with ChatGPT and other LLM-based writing tools, but with a key difference: it's embedded inline wherever you write rather than requiring a separate chat interface. Enterprise customers use Grammarly Business for brand tone consistency, compliance checking, and style guide enforcement across organizations. Its evolution from grammar checker to AI writing platform mirrors the broader trend of traditional software companies integrating LLM capabilities into existing products — a pattern explored across our [AI tooling coverage](/blog/claude-code-ctrl-s-prompt-stashing).

## How Grammarly Works

Grammarly operates through a layered architecture combining classical NLP with neural models:

- **Rule-based engine**: Handles deterministic grammar and punctuation corrections with high precision
- **Statistical models**: Trained on large corpora to detect clarity issues, wordiness, and passive voice
- **LLM integration**: GrammarlyGO uses large language models for generative rewrites, tone shifting, and contextual suggestions
- **Tone detection**: Classifies writing across dimensions like formality, confidence, and friendliness using [fine-tuned](/glossary/fine-tuning) classifiers

The system processes text client-side for basic checks and server-side for advanced AI features. Enterprise deployments support custom style guides and domain-specific vocabulary, allowing organizations to enforce consistent writing standards at scale.

## Related Terms

- **[ChatGPT](/glossary/chatgpt)**: OpenAI's conversational AI that competes with Grammarly's generative writing features through a chat-based interface
- **[Fine-Tuning](/glossary/fine-tuning)**: The technique Grammarly uses to adapt base language models for specialized writing tasks like tone detection
- **[Cursor](/glossary/cursor)**: An AI-enhanced code editor — analogous to Grammarly but for software development rather than prose
